The Artists

Noel Nicolas

“When I paint, the beast within is set free upon the canvas”

Noel is a graduate of BS in Interior Design at University of the East. He worked as a designer in a textile company for 14 years before he finally decided to work full time as freelance visual artist. Noel creates works that inspire quiet wonder. The abstract, minimalist creations balance nature`s flux in an expression of ordered chaos. A refiner of sorts, Noel engages in a-kind-of hybrid artistic and graphic enquiry to distill nature down to its primary core. Celebrating nature at its most unexpected, Noels` abstract works invite you to revel in their pure poetic forms, or decipher their origins- the beauty of it is left up to you.

Ayen Quias

“I am born with a talent and I’ll die with it…but I will not let it die with me”

The artist behind the Cheeks On Fleek. For Ayen Quias, making a painting is a process of discovery, a prolonged method of experimentation and transformation formed and molded by mixes of emotions of happiness and even depression leaving a soul that lingers and trapped inside each and every piece of her artworks. Finding inspiration from world-renowned painter Pablo Picasso, Van Gogh and Klimt Gustav, artist Ayen Quias has created her own line of figurative abstract works. Her subject are ladies with their cheeks on "fleeks", The distorted figures of her Madonna's are a representation of the hidden qualities of her character, that behind her strong personality is a soft and very sensitive lady unknown to many. A clearer understanding of her in an abstract form but none of it was intentional-it all developed and evolved over time. She has found inspiration and even solace in the fact that we maintain a steady course of change and growth. In 2013 she joined the Art Expo Malaysia and MASA group exhibit in Philippine Embassy in Malaysia. Being active in the art scene in the Philippines, Ayen Quias founded an art group named Contemporary Art Movement of the Philippines (CAMP) . Her advocacy is to help promote arts in the Philippines and to share culture to other artist internationally. In 2015 Ayen Quias works were exhibited at the One World Institute in Bitonto, Bari, Italy. and at the same year in Guanajuato, Mexico. One of her piece was included in one of the biggest art auction in the ASEAN held at Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. Her masterpiece “Sopistikada” is now a collection of New York modern art gallery.
